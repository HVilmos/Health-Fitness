BMI calculator



If your BMI is below 18.5, it is considered underweight according to the World Health Organization. This can increase your risk of health issues such as nutrient deficiencies and a weakened immune system. To achieve a healthy weight, it is important to focus on a balanced diet and regular exercise routine, and consulting with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian can also be helpful.

If your BMI falls between 18.5 and 25, it is considered within the healthy range according to the World Health Organization. However, it is still important to maintain a healthy diet and exercise routine to prevent health issues and maintain overall well-being. Regular check-ups with a healthcare professional can also help monitor your BMI and overall health.

If your BMI falls between 25 and 30, it is considered overweight according to the World Health Organization. This can increase your risk of health issues such as diabetes, heart disease, and high blood pressure. It is important to focus on a balanced diet and regular exercise routine to achieve a healthy weight. Consulting with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian can also be helpful in developing a personalized plan to achieve a healthy BMI.

If your BMI is above 30, it is considered obese according to the World Health Organization. This significantly increases your risk of health issues such as heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and certain cancers. It is important to take steps to achieve a healthy weight through a combination of a balanced diet and regular exercise. Consulting with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian can also be helpful in developing a personalized plan to achieve a healthy BMI and reduce the risk of associated health issues.

BMI stands for Body Mass Index, which is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It is commonly used to classify individuals as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese, and can be an indicator of potential health risks associated with weight.

  • BMI less than 18.5: Underweight
  • BMI between 18.5 and 24.9: Normal weight
  • BMI between 25 and 29.9: Overweight
  • BMI 30 or greater: Obese
